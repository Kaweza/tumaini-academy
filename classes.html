School Classes ‚Äì Early Learning Programs

Build a brighter future for your child with our structured and nurturing learning programs at Tumaini's Academy.

Baby (play ground)

The Playground Level is designed for young children around 3 to 4 years old. At this stage, the focus is on interactive learning through play. Activities are tailored to develop their motor skills, language abilities, and social interactions. Children are introduced to the basics of learning in a fun and engaging environment, helping them build a strong foundation for their educational journey

Middle

The Middle Level caters to children aged 4 to 5 years, a crucial stage for cognitive and emotional development. At this level, children begin to refine their language, math, and motor skills. Teachers use structured play and educational activities to support holistic development, focusing on nurturing the child's overall growth and preparing them for the more structured learning environment of junior Kindergarten.

Junior KG (Kindergarten)

Junior Kindergarten (Junior KG) is for children aged 5 to 6 years, where learning becomes more focused on academic readiness. The emphasis is on building early literacy, numeracy, and problem-solving skills. In this stage, children also begin to develop social skills and emotional intelligence, preparing them for the transition into formal schooling while still engaging in playful and creative learning experiences.

Senior KG (Kindergarten)

Senior Kindergarten, for children aged 6 to 7 years, takes the foundational skills learned in Junior KG and builds upon them. At this stage, children deepen their understanding of language, mathematics, and basic science concepts. Social and emotional skills are also developed, with a focus on preparing children for primary school. Structured learning becomes more prominent, but it still includes interactive and hands-on activities that engage the child‚Äôs imagination and curiosity.

Primary (clas I - VI)

Primary school encompasses children aged 7 to 12 years, from Class I to Class VI. The curriculum becomes more structured and academic, with a focus on subjects like English, Math, Science, and Social Studies. At this level, children begin to develop critical thinking, problem-solving, and communication skills. The primary years are essential for laying the foundation for future academic success, promoting both intellectual growth and personal development.
